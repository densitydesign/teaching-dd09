STUDY #1

AUSTRALIAN STUDY OF CHILD
HEALTH IN SAME-SEX FAMILIES

ON 500 CHILDREN AGED 0-17 YEARS
FROM VARIOUS FAMILY ORIGINS

On measures of general health and family cohesion, in the face of discrimination, children with lgbt parents showed a significantly better score when compared to Australian children from all backgrounds and family contexts. For all other health measures there were no statistically significant differences.

STUDY #1

NEW FAMILY STRUCTURE STUDY

ON 2988 YOUNG ADULTS AGED 18-39 YEAR
OF PARENTS IN A SAME-SEX RELATIONSHIP

There are differences between children raised by a parent who had a same-sex relationship and children raised in an intact, biological, married family when it comes to social, emotional and relational outcomes. The young-adult children of lesbian mothers exhibited the least favorable outcomes in a wider array of categories when compared to the children of intact, biological families and fared worse in more categories than the children of gay fathers.

      NATURAL INTERCOURSE

      Lgbt people can have children both from heterosexual intercourses and from former relationships.Being lgbt doesn’t mean that you can’t have children by sexual relations.

      DONOR INSEMINATION

      Is one method used from lgbt people who wish to have a biological child, meaning that at least one of the parents is a biological one. Lesbians may look for a sperm donor, while gays for an egg donor (and a surrogate mother)the provider can be known or anonymous.

      IN VITRO FERTILIZATION

      Is a process by which an egg is fertilised by sperm outside the body: in vitro. Is a treatment used when other methods or assisted reproductive techniques have failed or are not possible. For male parents is the only way to be biological parents.

      SURROGACY

      Is an arrangement in which a woman carries and delivers a child for anyone else. The surrogate may be the child genetic mother (traditional surrogacy) or not (gestational surrogacy), so must work through in vitro fertilization. Monetary compensation may or may not be involved in the arrangements.

      ADOPTION

      Is a process whereby a person assumes the parenting for a child and permanently transfers all rights and responsibilities from the biological parent or parents. Adoption is intended to effect a permanent change in status and as such requires societal recognition, either through legal or religious sanction.

      FOSTER CARE

      Is the term used for a system in which a minor has been placed with a state-certified caregiver. The placement of the child is usually arranged through the government or a social-service agency. The institution, group home or foster parent is compensated for expenses.
